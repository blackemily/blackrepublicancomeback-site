School 2 · History, Economics & Civic Truth

Understand the story and the incentives beneath it.

This school walks you through the parts of history and economics most people never really learn: the Reconstruction era and Black Republicans, how parties and narratives shifted, how incentives actually work, and what the Constitution and government are supposed to be doing.

You don’t have to agree with every conclusion to benefit. The goal here is context and clarity, not obedience.
What this school helps you do

Instead of memorizing disconnected dates and slogans, you’ll see how events, incentives, and institutions fit together — especially around Reconstruction and its aftermath.

  • Get a clear, narrative overview of Reconstruction and the Black Republican era.
  • Understand basic economics and why incentives quietly drive so many outcomes.
  • Learn how the U.S. government is structured so you’re harder to mislead.
Built for readers & non-readers Ask questions, get explanations, work at your own pace.
The three pillars of this school
Everything here lives under three big headings: history with context, economics with real-world examples, and civic structure without the fog.
Pillar 1
History with Reconstruction at the center

Instead of racing from the Founding to the present in one blurry line, this pillar slows down around Reconstruction and the wave of Black Republicans who stepped into public life.

  • What Reconstruction actually was and why it mattered.
  • How Black Republicans gained office, then were slowly pushed out.
  • How narratives about parties and race shifted over time.
Pillar 2
Economics & incentives in plain language

No graphs required. You’ll learn to see the incentives under policies, programs, and predictions — and how those incentives shape behavior more than speeches do.

  • Basic ideas: supply & demand, inflation, trade, taxes.
  • Why “free” things usually have a hidden price.
  • How economic rules show up in everyday life decisions.
Pillar 3
Civic truth: how the system is supposed to work

You’ll get a clear map of the Constitution, the branches of government, and what each part is supposed to be doing — before we talk about what’s actually happening now.

  • Constitution basics: structures, limits, and rights.
  • Congress, the Presidency, and the courts in simple terms.
  • Why checks and balances exist — and what it looks like when they weaken.
How your learning path works
You can treat this school like a casual tutoring stop or like a structured course. These three “levels” describe how deep you want to go.
Level 1 · Free foundations
Quick clarity sessions

Use the tutor for short sessions: “Explain Reconstruction like I’m 15,” “What is inflation?”, “What does the House actually do?” You get clean, simple explanations with room for follow-up questions.

Level 2 · Guided path
Storyline learning

When you’re ready, you can walk through a more ordered path: start with a timeline of Reconstruction, move into basic economics, then finish with a clear map of civic structure. The tutor can keep track of where you are and what you’ve covered.

Level 3 · Deep dives (optional)
Books, policy debates, and nuance

For deeper study, you can bring in books, articles, and speeches. The tutor can help you summarize, compare claims, and see how different writers frame the same events and policies.

What the tutor will do

The tutor is designed to be conversational, patient, and structured. You can ask broad questions or dig into details. You can also say what level you’re at (“I never paid attention in history class,” “I know some econ but not much”) and it will adjust.

  • Explain events, terms, and ideas in clear, plain language.
  • Help you build timelines and cause–effect chains.
  • Compare different interpretations of the same event or policy.
  • Relate history and economics back to incentives and human behavior.
