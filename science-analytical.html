What you’ll learn here
We focus on big ideas, not memorized trivia. The point is to understand how the world works well enough to reason about claims, news, and everyday problems.
Physics & the physical world
How things move, push, pull, and break

You’ll learn core ideas like forces, energy, motion, and fields — the invisible rules that explain why cars move, planes fly, and bridges don’t (usually) collapse.

  • Forces, motion, and energy — in normal language.
  • Gravity, orbits, and why things fall the way they do.
  • Electricity and magnetism as everyday phenomena, not just equations.
Biology & living systems
How life is organized and stays alive

You’ll explore cells, organs, ecosystems, and the logic of how living things maintain themselves, adapt, and sometimes fail.

  • Cells, tissues, organs, and systems — as a connected story.
  • How information and energy move through living things.
  • Immune systems, health, and what “normal” looks like for the body.
Chemistry & matter
What everything is made of and how it changes

You’ll learn about atoms, molecules, reactions, and states of matter — how substances combine, separate, and transform.

  • Atoms, bonds, and why some combinations are stable and others explode.
  • States of matter, solutions, and everyday reactions (like rust or cooking).
  • How chemical ideas apply to food, fuel, and materials you use every day.
Scientific method & data
How evidence actually works

You’ll practice thinking like someone who runs experiments: testable ideas, evidence, error, and honest conclusions — plus basic data and spreadsheet work.

  • Hypotheses, experiments, variables, and controls.
  • Correlation vs. causation, and why that matters so much.
  • Basic spreadsheets: sorting, simple formulas, and reading charts.
Core modules inside this school
These are the main “tracks” you can work through. You can do them in order, or jump straight to the one that solves a problem you have right now.
Four main learning tracks
Each track can be taken as slowly or quickly as you like. The goal is not to impress anyone — it’s to understand and be able to explain what you’ve learned.
  • Track 1 – Conceptual Physics
    Motion, forces, energy, and fields, explained with real-world examples (cars, sports, buildings) and simple diagrams.
  • Track 2 – Conceptual Biology
    Cells, systems, health, and ecology, with an emphasis on how your own body and environment actually work.
  • Track 3 – Conceptual Chemistry
    Matter, molecules, reactions, and everyday chemistry in cooking, cleaning, fuel, and materials.
  • Track 4 – Scientific Thinking & Data
    How to form testable ideas, read science news with skepticism, and use basic spreadsheets to explore data and patterns.
How this connects to the bigger comeback

A serious comeback for a country requires more than slogans. It needs people who can understand evidence, see through bad data presentations, and resist being pushed around by scare stories dressed up as “science.”

This school is here so that ordinary people — not just specialists — can look at claims about health, energy, climate, technology, or risk and say: “I actually understand enough to ask better questions.”

You don’t have to become a scientist. But you do have to stop being easy to fool.
